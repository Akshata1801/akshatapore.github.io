My Background
With a Master's in Robotics Engineering from Worcester Polytechnic Institute, I have honed my expertise in computer vision through various practical experiences and research roles. During my internship at MathWorks, I worked extensively with MATLAB and Simulink to test and analyze robotics cases, contributing to the Robotics Toolbox. At Southie Autonomy, as a Perception Engineer, I focused on advancing object detection techniques for pick-and-place robots by leveraging 3D Point Cloud processing (PCL, C++) and image processing (OpenCV, C++). My work included designing object detection systems with camera and LiDAR, which led to a 30% improvement in performance by optimizing a key subprocess.
In my research role at the Berk Callis lab, I trained a deep Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) using PyTorch to estimate joint angles from depth images of a KUKA robot, achieving 85% accuracy. I also implemented a path planning algorithm for autonomous robot movement and depth image capture (C++, ROS). Additionally, I applied advanced computer vision techniques, such as Driveable Area detection using a model inspired by the "Attention Is All You Need" paper and object classification with the Swin Transformer on the COCO dataset, showcasing my ability to implement state-of-the-art models in practical applications.
